The Buying Process

5 steps to finding your perfect home

1. Get Pre-Approved

Frequently Asked Questions

How much home can I afford?

Generally, lenders will approve you for a mortgage of 2.5 to 3 times your annual income. Your actual budget depends on your down payment, credit score, debt-to-income ratio, and current interest rates. Angela recommends getting pre-approved first to understand your true buying power.

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is a quick estimate based on self-reported information. Pre-approval is a detailed review by a lender that verifies your income, credit, and assets. Pre-approval is what sellers take seriously when reviewing offers.

How long does the home buying process take?

From offer to closing typically takes 30-45 days. However, this depends on how quickly inspections, appraisals, and underwriting are completed. Some deals close faster, others take longer.

What should I look for in a home inspection?

A professional inspector will examine the structure, roof, foundation, electrical systems, plumbing, HVAC, and more. Angela recommends attending the inspection to ask questions and understand the condition of the property.

What are typical closing costs?

Closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the purchase price and include loan origination fees, appraisal, title insurance, taxes, and attorney fees. Angela will provide a detailed estimate after you make an offer.
